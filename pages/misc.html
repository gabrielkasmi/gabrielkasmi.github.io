Contribution to research projects

Osmose H2020: Leveraging flexibilities for the European power system

During my apprenticeship at RTE France (2019-2020), I contributed to the work package 2 of the European research project Osmose. I contributed to the development of the ATLAS-PROMETHEUS market simulation model. This agent-based model takes into account the uncertainties that the market agent face when formulating orders to the market. These uncertainties mostly revolve around the renewables output and load output. More precisely, my work consisted in developping the module in charge of the formulation of the orders by the market agents on the day-ahead market. This induced the development and implementation of an optimization program based on existing literature for the formmulation of orders from thermic units. You can access the documentation of the ATLAS-PROMETHEUS model here and my apprenticeship report here (in French).

Master thesis

Prior to ENSAE, I graduated from the Analysis and policy in Economics master, at the Paris School of Economics. As part of this master I wrote a dissertation on the French MP's, under the aegis of Prof. Ekaterina Zhuravskaya. You can access the manuscript here.

In this dissertation, I leveraged the data gathered by the non-profit NosDeputés.fr, which collects data on the activity on the French MP's since 2009. Data features the transcripts of the pleniary and commission sessions, amendments, reports. Attendence of the MPs to the pleniaries and their commission sessions is deduced from the raw data. Using this data, I created a dataset to see whether the behavior of the MPs shifts when they are in the majority or the opposition. To analyse the causal effect of belonging to the majority I use a diff-and-diff estimator and leverage the majority shift that occured in 2012. Results showed that when in the majority, a MP tend to be more active in commissions and when in the oppisition, he will talk more often during plenary sessions.
